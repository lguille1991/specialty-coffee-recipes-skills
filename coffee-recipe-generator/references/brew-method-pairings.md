# Brew Method Recommendations by Coffee Profile

Use this section to recommend the best brew method(s) for a given coffee, or to help a user choose a coffee for their preferred brew method.

## How to Read This Section

Two workflows:
1. **"I have this coffee — what method suits it?"** — Match the coffee's origin, process, and variety to the recommendations below.
2. **"I have this brew method — what coffee should I use?"** — Reverse-lookup which origins/profiles work best with each method.

## Brew Method Profiles (what each method is best at)

| Brew Method | Strengths | Weaknesses | Best Coffee Profile |
|-------------|-----------|------------|---------------------|
| **V60 / Pour-Over (conical)** | Brightness, clarity, complex aromatics, terroir expression | Requires technique; less body | Washed, light-medium roast, high-grown, acidic coffees |
| **Chemex** | Clean cup, sweetness, smooth body, low acidity | Slower drawdown; thick filters remove some oils | Natural process, medium roast, fruit-forward coffees |
| **Kalita / Flat-Bottom** | Forgiving, consistent, balanced | Less bright than V60 | Any profile; great all-rounder especially washed |
| **French Press** | Full body, rich mouthfeel, oils preserved | Muddy, sediment, no clarity | Brazil, Sumatra, medium-dark roast, natural/honey |
| **AeroPress** | Versatile, portable, forgiving of technique | Less suited to showcasing delicate notes | Any profile; especially good for experimenting |
| **Origami / Cupsmith** | Clean, sweet, nuanced | Similar to V60 but less widely documented | Washed, light-medium roast, floral coffees |
| **Moka Pot** | Strong, concentrated, espresso-like | Bitter if mishandled; no clarity | Darker roast, full-bodied coffees |
| **Siphon** | Clean, bright, tea-like elegance | Fragile; requires technique and equipment | Delicate high-grown washed coffees, Gesha |
| **Cold Brew** | Sweet, smooth, low acidity, high caffeine | Requires 12-24 hours; no aromatics | Medium-dark roast, Brazilian/Colombian, natural/honey |

## Best Brew Methods by Origin

| Origin | Best Methods (in priority order) | Why |
|--------|--------------------------------|-----|
| **Ethiopia** | V60, Chemex, Siphon, Kalita | Brightness and floral aromatics shine in paper filters; lets terroir speak |
| **Kenya** | V60, Kalita, Chemex | Berry and citrus brightness is best expressed in pour-overs |
| **Colombia** | V60, Kalita, French Press, AeroPress | Versatile; washed Colombians are clean and balanced in any method |
| **Brazil** | French Press, Moka Pot, Cold Brew, Chemex | Low acidity + chocolate/nut = natural fit for full-body methods |
| **Guatemala** | V60, Kalita, French Press | Chocolate and spice notes come through in pour-overs; fuller body in immersion |
| **Yemen** | V60, Chemex (low temp) | Wild, wine-like coffees need a method that preserves fruit complexity without burning aromatics |
| **Panama (Gesha)** | V60, Siphon, Kalita (low temp) | Delicate florals are volatile; lower temps and paper filters preserve aromatics |
| **Costa Rica** | V60, Kalita, AeroPress | Honey and citrus brightness suits pour-overs |
| **Rwanda** | V60, Siphon, Kalita | Floral and lemon clarity suits paper-filter methods |
| **El Salvador (Pacamara)** | V60, Chemex, Kalita | Large beans + floral/stone fruit = best expressed in pour-overs for clarity |
| **Indonesia (Sumatra)** | French Press, Moka Pot, Cold Brew | Earthy, full-body coffees need immersion to extract and preserve character |

## Best Brew Methods by Processing Method

| Process | Best Methods (in priority order) | Rationale |
|---------|--------------------------------|-----------|
| **Washed** | V60, Kalita, Siphon, Chemex | Clean cup allows terroir and variety to shine; no fruit interference |
| **Natural** | Chemex, French Press, Cold Brew | Thick paper filters (Chemex) manage intensity; immersion (French Press) handles heavy body |
| **Honey** | V60, Kalita, Origami | Balance of sweetness and clarity; pour-overs don't overpower the delicate mucilage character |
| **Anaerobic / Experimental** | V60, AeroPress (low temp) | These coffees are more soluble — lower temps (88-93°C) and shorter brew times prevent over-extraction. See Experimental Processing section below. |
| **Carbonic Maceration** | V60, Chemex (low temp) | Wine-like, boozy qualities come through best in pour-overs at lower temperatures |
| **Thermal Shock / Frozen** | V60, Chemex | High intensity processing needs lower temp to balance; paper filters tame extremes |

## Best Brew Methods by Roast Level

| Roast Level | Best Methods | Rationale |
|-------------|-------------|-----------|
| **Light** | V60, Siphon, Kalita | High extraction ability of pour-overs handles dense light-roast beans; lower yield potential |
| **Medium** | V60, Kalita, Chemex, AeroPress | Balanced extraction; all methods work well |
| **Medium-Dark** | Kalita, Chemex, French Press | Slightly lower temp needed; full-body methods compensate for lost brightness |
| **Dark** | French Press, Moka Pot, Cold Brew | Immersion and pressure extract better from less-dense dark beans; oils preserved |

## Experimental / Anaerobic Processing — Special Handling

Experimental coffees (anaerobic fermentation, carbonic maceration, malic fermentation, koji mould, etc.) break down sugars faster, making them **more soluble** than traditionally processed coffees. This means they extract at a faster rate and are easier to over-extract.

**Key adjustments:**
- **Temperature:** Use lower water temps — 88-93°C (190-200°F). Never go above 95°C.
- **Brew time:** Shorter total brew time (2:00-3:00 for pour-overs instead of 2:30-3:30).
- **Grind:** Slightly coarser than you would for a washed coffee of the same origin.
- **Pour strategy:** Use gentler pours to avoid over-agitation and channeling.

**Recipe example (Elika Liftee / Onyx Coffee Lab — 2022 US Brewers Cup Champion):**
- 15g medium-fine ground coffee
- 250ml water at 93°C
- 50g bloom, wait 20s
- At 0:20: pour 50g
- At 0:50: aggressively pour 75g
- At 1:20: aggressively pour 75g
- Target total brew time: 2:30-3:00

**Two-kettle method (Sam Corra / ONA Coffee):**
- Use two kettles: one at 92°C, one at 88°C
- Start with the hotter water, finish with cooler water to protect volatile aromatics
- Reduces tannin extraction, minimizing bitterness and dryness

## Cold Brew — Coffee Selection Guide

Cold brew's long immersion (8-24 hours) at room temp or below creates a sweet, low-acid, high-caffeine cup. The extended extraction time mitigates acidity and bitterness, amplifying natural sweetness.

| Factor | Recommendation |
|--------|---------------|
| **Origin** | Central/South America (Brazil, Colombia, Guatemala) for chocolate-nut profiles; East Africa (Ethiopia, Kenya) for fruit-forward; Indonesia for earthy-spicy |
| **Process** | Natural or dark honey for sweetness; washed for brighter, cleaner cold brew |
| **Roast** | Medium to medium-dark — darker roasts extract more sweetness but too dark = bitter. Medium roasts hit the sweet spot |
| **Grind** | Coarse (coarser than French Press — typical cold brew grind is very coarse) |
| **Water Temp** | Room temp or cold — no heat needed |
| **Steep Time** | 12-24 hours; longer = more body but risk of bitterness |
| **Note** | K-Ultra reaches cold brew only at its maximum setting, and Q Air works best near the coarse end of its range. Treat both as practical/marginal options for cold brew rather than fully adjustable dedicated coarse grinders. |

## Reverse Lookup: "I have [brew method] — what coffee should I use?"

| Brew Method | Ideal Coffee Characteristics |
|------------|------------------------------|
| **V60** | Washed, light-medium roast, high-grown, acidic. Ethiopia, Kenya, Panama Gesha, Rwanda |

| **French Press** | Medium-dark roast, low-acidity, full body. Brazil, Sumatra, Guatemala dark |
| **AeroPress** | Any — especially forgiving for beginner-friendly brewing or experimentation |
| **Origami** | Washed, light-medium roast, floral. Ethiopia washed, Costa Rica, Colombia |
| **Moka Pot** | Medium-dark roast, full-bodied. Brazil, Colombia, Sumatra |
| **Siphon** | Delicate, high-grown, washed. Panama Gesha, Ethiopia Yirgacheffe, Rwanda |
| **Cold Brew** | Medium-dark roast, natural or honey process, sweet. Brazil, Colombia, Ethiopia natural |

## Quick Decision Flowchart (text-based)

```
START: What is your priority for this cup?
|
├─ Clarity + Brightness → Is the coffee washed + high-grown?
|   ├─ YES → V60 or Kalita
|   └─ NO (natural/honey) → Chemex
|
├─ Body + Richness → Is it a dark roast or Brazilian/Indonesian?
|   ├─ YES → French Press or Moka Pot
|   └─ NO → Try Kalita + longer brew time
|
├─ Fruit + Intensity → Is it experimental or natural processed?
|   ├─ YES → V60 or Chemex at lower temp (91-93°C)
|   └─ NO → Kalita standard approach
|
├─ Floral + Delicate → Is it Gesha or another delicate variety?
|   ├─ YES → Siphon or V60 at 90-93°C
|   └─ NO → Kalita standard approach
|
└─ Convenience + Volume → Cold Brew or Batch Brew
    ├─ Cold Brew → Medium-dark, natural, Brazilian/Colombian
    └─ Batch Brew → Kalita for consistency, V60 for quality
```
