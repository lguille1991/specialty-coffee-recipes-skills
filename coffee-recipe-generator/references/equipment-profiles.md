**When you want maximum clarity and brightness:** V60, Orea V4 Wide, Origami Air M
- Best with: washed Ethiopia, Kenya, Panama Gesha, Rwanda

**When you want sweetness and fruit expression:** Chemex, NextLevel Pulsar
- Best with: natural Ethiopia/Colombia, honey process, anaerobic lots

**When you want a reliable daily driver:** Kalita Wave Mino 185, Ceado Hoop
- Best with: washed or honey, any origin, medium roast

**When you want full body and richness:** AeroPress XL (inverted/full immersion)
- Best with: Brazil, Sumatra, medium-dark roast, Indonesian coffees

**When you want to experiment or travel light:** AeroPress Classic
- Best with: any coffee, especially espresso-style brews or on-the-go

## Your Brew Methods — Design-Specific Insights

Understanding *why* each brewer pairs with certain profiles:

**Chemex** — Thick bonded paper filters (20-30% heavier than standard) remove fine particles and most oils, producing an exceptionally clean, sweet cup. The slow drawdown (4-8 min for 3-cup) allows extended water-coffee contact, making it ideal for coffees where you want to tame intense fruit without losing sweetness. Best used at 93-96°C for medium roasts; drop to 90-93°C for naturals.

**Hario V60 02** — 60° conical angle and large single hole create a fast, radial flow that highlights acidity and aromatic complexity. The design favours coffees with something to say — high-grown washed lots, delicate varieties, experimental processes. Less ideal for coffees that need body or taming.

**Hario Switch 03** — Identical footprint to the V60 but with a valve at the bottom that allows immersion brewing. Valve closed = immersion steep; valve open = V60-style drawdown/release. The hybrid nature makes it the most versatile dripper here — great for coffees where you want the clarity of paper filters but with the body and sweetness that immersion adds.

**AeroPress Classic / XL** — Uses air pressure to accelerate extraction, producing a clean, concentrated cup regardless of grind size. The short brew time (1-3 min) minimises acidity extraction, making it forgiving with dark roasts and low-acid coffees. The XL handles up to ~30g doses for bigger batches.

**Kalita Wave Mino 185** — Your current Kalita. Flat-bottom ceramic Wave brewer that uses the same Kalita 185 Wave filters as the older ceramic 185, but with a faster-flowing bottom design. Kalita USA confirms the ceramic, flat-bottom 3-hole design and 185 Wave filter compatibility; Kurasu notes the MI/Mino holes are about 2 mm larger than standard Wave; Coffee Chronicler reports the 185 Mino Yaki has a redesigned raised bottom and larger 4 mm drain holes for faster, more consistent flow with fewer stalls. Generate Mino recipes with medium-fine grind, 3-4 pulse pours, thorough ceramic preheat, and a 2:45-3:15 target rather than old slow-Kalita timings.

**NextLevel Pulsar** — A hybrid brewer combining immersion and pour-over elements. Water is poured over the bed and flows through by gravity, with the design encouraging even saturation. Particularly effective at expressing the fruit character of natural and honey processed coffees. Requires dial-in but rewards experimentation.

**Ceado Hoop** — Radial infusion brewer: water enters through side holes at the base of the Flow Tower and spreads evenly across the coffee bed. This creates uniform extraction without the technique demands of manual pour-overs. The paper filter sits in the Outer Loop, and the whole assembly is technique-simple — pour, wait, serve. Maximum 20g dose. Best for everyday brewing rather than competition-level expression.

**Orea V4 Wide** — Flat-bottom brewer with patented "tilted ribs" that sit under the filter, creating channels for even water distribution. The wide format (for 185+ filters) creates a thin, uniform bed that minimises channelling. The Orea is explicitly competition-grade and best at expressing washed delicate coffees and experimental processes where every gram of extraction matters. For bottom inserts, filter pairings, competition recipes, and source notes, load `references/orea-v4-research.md`; keep that file as the canonical Orea V4 source to avoid drift.

**Origami Air M** — Mino porcelain cone (like V60) but with 20 evenly-spaced ribs and a large base opening. Works with both conical filters (faster flow, more agitation) and Kalita Wave flat-bottom filters (slower flow, more uniform extraction). The large opening and 20-rib geometry minimise bypass and enhance sweetness. The plastic Origami Air version heats up faster and is preferred for competition; porcelain retains heat better for everyday use.
