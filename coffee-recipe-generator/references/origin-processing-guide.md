# Coffee Origin & Processing Guidance

## Processing Method Effects (Critical for Recipe Parameters)

| Process | Temp Guide | Grind | Brew Time | Flavor Character |
|---------|-----------|-------|-----------|------------------|
| **Washed** | 93-96°C (higher) | Medium-fine | Standard | Clean, bright, acidic, terroir-forward |
| **Natural** | 91-94°C (lower) | Medium-coarse | Longer | Fruity, wine-like, heavy body, intense |
| **Honey** | 92-95°C (mid) | Medium | Standard-mid | Sweet, balanced, silky, complex |

## Variety-Specific Notes
- **Gesha/Geisha:** Use lower temps (90-93°C), delicate, floral compounds are volatile
- **Bourbon:** Standard approach, good sweetness
- **Typica:** Standard approach, clean and balanced
- **SL28/SL34 (Kenya):** Higher temp okay, handles extraction well
- **Caturra/Cataui:** Standard approach, forgiving
- **Pacamara:** Large bean size inherited from Maragogipe — use standard grind but expect slower drawdown due to larger particles. Excellent with washed processing; can handle 93-95°C. Shows strong floral and stone fruit notes. Susceptible to leaf rust so quality varies by farm. Best brewed as pour-over (V60, Chemex) to highlight its clarity and acidity.

## Roast Level Adjustments
- **Light roast:** Use higher temp (95-96°C), finer grind may help extraction
- **Medium roast:** Standard temp (93-95°C)
- **Medium-dark roast:** Use slightly lower temp (91-94°C), coarser grind may help
