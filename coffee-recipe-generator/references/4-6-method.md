# Tetsu Kasuya's 4:6 Method (V60)

The 4:6 Method was created by **Tetsu Kasuya** to win the **2016 World Brewers Cup**. It is designed as a **universal, foolproof system** that controls flavor through simple number splits rather than advanced pouring technique.

## Core Philosophy
- Split total brew water into **40%** and **60%**
- **First 40%** (first 2 pours) controls the **balance between acidity and sweetness**
- **Last 60%** (remaining 3 pours) controls **concentration / strength**
- Start each next pour only when water in the dripper has **almost completely drained** — Kasuya calls this timing "a hidden discovery" of the method

## Standard Recipe Parameters
- **Coffee:** 20g
- **Total water:** 300g
- **Ratio:** 1:15
- **Number of pours:** 5 equal pours of **60g each**
- **Water temperature:** varies by roast level:
  - Light roast: **93-94°C**
  - Medium roast: **87-88°C**
  - Dark roast: **83°C**
- **Grind:** **Medium-coarse** (Kasuya explicitly calls it "coarse grinding")
- **Total brew time target:** **3:30 to 4:00 maximum**

## Grind Calibration (Time-Based Test)
Because "medium-coarse" is vague, Kasuya gives a time-based test:
- If water drains **before 3 minutes** → grind is **too coarse**
- If water takes **longer than 3:30-4:00** → grind is **too fine**
- Ideal: water finishes draining around **3:30**

## The Exact Steps
1. Rinse paper filter with hot water to preheat dripper and server.
2. Add **20g medium-coarse ground coffee**.
3. **Pour 1 (Bloom):** 60g at **0s**. Wait until water has nearly drained.
4. **Pour 2:** 60g at **~45s** (total 120g = **40%**). Wait until nearly drained.
5. **Pour 3:** 60g at **~1m30s**. Wait until nearly drained.
6. **Pour 4:** 60g at **~2m15s**. Wait until nearly drained.
7. **Pour 5:** 60g at **~3m00s** (total 300g = **100%**). Let it finish drawing down.

> **Note on intervals:** The 4:6 method is fundamentally a **drain-timed** system, not a clock-timed one. The exact intervals above (~45s between pours) assume a calibrated medium-coarse grind and a 20g dose. If your drawdown is faster or slower, intervals shift accordingly. Kasuya's core rule is "pour the next dose only when water is almost completely drained." These times are a reference for a correctly calibrated grind.

## Drawdown Timing Targets
- **Remove dripper / end of brew:**
  - Light strength (2 pours in 60% phase): **~2m15s**
  - Medium strength (3 pours in 60% phase): **~3m00s**
  - Strong strength (5 total pours): **~3m45s**
- Total brew time should not exceed **3:30–4:00**; if it does, grind coarser.

## What Each Pour Does

### The First 40% (Pours 1 + 2 = 120g)
Controls the **balance between acidity and sweetness**:

| First Pour | Second Pour | Result |
|------------|-------------|--------|
| **50g** | **70g** | **More sweetness** |
| **60g** | **60g** | **Balanced** (Kasuya's recommendation) |
| **70g** | **50g** | **More acidity / brightness** |

### The Last 60% (Pours 3-5 = 180g)
Controls **concentration / strength**:

| Number of pours | Amount per pour | Result |
|-----------------|-----------------|--------|
| **3 pours** | 60g × 3 | **Rich / concentrated** |
| **2 pours** | 90g × 2 | **Refreshing** |
| **1 pour** | 180g all at once | **Very light / Americano-like** |

## What Coffee Beans Benefit Most
Kasuya states: **"Any kind of coffee will become delicious."** The method is deliberately universal. However, it particularly shines with:
- **Washed coffees** — clean extraction highlights terroir and acidity
- **High-grown / dense beans** — coarse grind + near-drain technique manages density without over-extraction
- **Light to medium roasts** — higher temp range (93°C+) and clean cup suit bright, complex coffees
- **Delicate varieties like Gesha** — clarity and lack of fines migration preserves floral notes

## Scaling to Larger Brews
**Yes, the method scales proportionally** because it is based on percentages (40/60 split).

- **V60 02** comfortable up to ~30-32g coffee / ~450-500ml water
- **V60 03, Chemex, Kalita 185** needed for 500ml+ batches
- Core principles (40/60 split, near-complete drainage between pours) remain the same
- As the bed gets deeper with larger doses, you may need to grind slightly coarser to maintain the 3:30-4:00 window

## Common 4:6 Mistakes
| Mistake | Result | Fix |
|---------|--------|-----|
| Pouring next pour before water drains | Weak, under-extracted cup | Wait until water is nearly gone before next pour |
| Using too fine a grind | Brew time exceeds 4:00, bitter | Go coarser; target 3:30 total |
| Using too coarse a grind | Brew finishes before 3:00, weak | Go finer; target 3:30 total |
| Ignoring roast-level temperature | Light roast tastes under-extracted at 87°C | Light roasts need 93-94°C; dark roasts need 83°C |
