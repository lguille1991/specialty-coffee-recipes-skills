# Brewer Version Adaptation Rules

> How to adapt recipes when switching between brewer versions, attachments, or similar geometries with different flow characteristics.

---

## General Adaptation Rules

When a brewer's drain speed or restriction changes, compensation must come from grind, pours, or time. These rules apply across all brewer swaps.

| Change You Made | Compensate With | Why |
|-----------------|-----------------|-----|
| **Faster drain / less restriction** | Grind finer, add more pours, extend steep | Faster drain = less contact time; you must create resistance |
| **Slower drain / more restriction** | Grind coarser, reduce pours, shorten steep | Slower drain = more contact time; avoid over-extraction |
| **Higher thermal mass (thick ceramic)** | Preheat thoroughly; start 1–2 °C cooler | Ceramic holds heat well; starting cooler prevents scorching |
| **Lower thermal mass (thin metal/glass)** | Preheat is critical; start 1–2 °C hotter | Thin metal loses heat fast; hotter start compensates |
| **Metal filter vs paper filter** | Metal = more oils/body; paper = cleaner/lighter | Adjust dose or ratio if body is too heavy/light |
| **Full immersion attachment (Prismo)** | Finer grind, longer steep, lower temp | Immersion extracts more; finer grind builds body without bitterness |

## Case Study 1: Kalita Wave Ceramic 185 / Classic Wave → Kalita Wave Mino 185

### What Changed

| Feature | Older Wave ceramic / classic steel | Mino 185 |
|---------|----------------------------------|----------|
| Bottom support | Older ceramic ribs / shallow steel ribs | Redesigned raised circular platform |
| Hole size | Smaller, more restrictive holes | Larger holes; Kurasu describes MI holes as 2 mm larger than standard Wave |
| Flow rate | Moderate to slow; steel often stalls | Fast, consistent, low-stall |
| Thermal mass | Ceramic high; steel low | High ceramic thermal mass |
| Filter | Kalita 185 Wave | Same Kalita 185 Wave |
| Clogging risk | Ceramic low; steel high on multi-pour | Very low |
| Typical dose | 15-25 g | 20-30 g sweet spot, still usable lower |

### Adaptation

| Parameter | Older Wave ceramic / classic steel | Mino 185 | Reason |
|-----------|----------------------------------|----------|--------|
| **Grind** | Medium to medium-fine | **Medium-fine**; start 1 click finer than old ceramic, 1-2 clicks finer than steel | Faster drain needs more resistance |
| **Pours** | 2-3 pulses often enough | **3-4 pulses** | More pulses add contact time without relying on stall |
| **Temperature** | 93-96 °C | **92-94 °C** | Ceramic heat retention and faster flow both matter; stay lower for delicate/processed coffees, hotter for dense washed lots |
| **Total time** | 3:00-3:45 depending on model | **2:45-3:15** | Mino should not be forced to old stalled Wave timings |
| **Preheat** | Rinse filter; ceramic benefits from warm-up | **Rinse + warm ceramic body thoroughly** | Thermal mass steals heat from the bloom if cold |
| **Filters** | Kalita 185 Wave | **Kalita 185 Wave** | Same filter size; recipe changes are driven by dripper flow, not filter compatibility |

### Flavor Impact
- **Older ceramic Wave:** Balanced and forgiving, but slower than the Mino; recipes may taste thin on the Mino unless grind/pulse count is adjusted.
- **Classic steel Wave:** Slower drain acts as built-in restrictor; more forgiving with coarse grind; can mute acidity but risks stalling.
- **Mino 185:** Fast, predictable drain preserves clarity; no stalling anxiety on multi-pour recipes; ceramic stabilizes temperature for more even extraction. If the cup is hollow, tighten grind before extending brew time.

### Source Notes
- Kalita USA: Mino 185 is ceramic, uses Kalita 185 Wave filters, has a flat-bottom 3-hole design, and recommends warming the dripper before brewing.
- Kurasu: MI/Mino dripper keeps the Wave flat-bottom flow-control design and has holes about 2 mm larger than standard Kalita Wave for a more flavorful, aromatic cup.
- Coffee Chronicler: 2025 Mino Yaki update uses a redesigned raised bottom and larger 4 mm drain holes, producing faster, more consistent flow and solving many previous stalling issues.

## Case Study 2: AeroPress Standard → AeroPress + Fellow Prismo

### What Changed

| Feature | Standard AeroPress | Prismo Attachment |
|---------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Filter | Paper (blocks oils) | Metal (allows oils) |
| Leakage | Drip-through during steep | Valve prevents drip-through |
| Geometry | Upright or inverted | Upright only (valve holds water) |
| Body | Clean, light | **Bold, syrupy** |
| Clarity | High | Lower (oils pass through) |

### Adaptation

| Parameter | Standard | Prismo | Reason |
|-----------|----------|--------|--------|
| **Grind** | Medium-fine | **Medium-fine to fine** | Full immersion + metal = more extraction; finer grind builds body |
| **Ratio** | 1:12 to 1:15 | **1:12 to 1:13** | Metal filter adds body; slightly stronger ratio preserves sweetness |
| **Temperature** | 80–93 °C | **85–89 °C** | Lower temp protects against bitterness when oils are present |
| **Steep time** | 1:30–2:00 | **2:00–2:30** | No drip-through means you can steep longer without losing strength |
| **Press** | Standard plunger | **Slow, steady 30–40 s** | Metal filter has less resistance; slow press prevents channeling |

### Flavor Impact
- **Standard:** Clean, bright, tea-like. Good for showcasing delicate aromatics.
- **Prismo:** Bold, oily, almost creamy. Good for naturals, honeys, and anyone wanting an immersion-style body from an AeroPress.

## Case Study 3: Bean Swap — Bold Variety → Delicate Variety (Same Brewer)

When keeping the brewer constant but changing the bean, the extraction target changes.

| From → To | Grind | Temp | Steep | Expectation |
|-----------|-------|------|-------|-------------|
| **Pacamara Black Honey → Geisha Natural** | Same or slightly finer | Same or 1 °C cooler | Same or slightly shorter | Geisha is more delicate; will not achieve the same syrupy body. Expect "elegant bold," not "punch in the mouth bold." |
| **Washed → Natural** | Same or slightly coarser | Same | Same or slightly shorter | Naturals have more ferment/body; coarser grind prevents muddiness |
| **Natural → Washed** | Same or slightly finer | Same or 1 °C hotter | Same or slightly longer | Washed is cleaner; needs more contact time to build equivalent sweetness |
| **Medium-light roast → Dark roast** | Coarser | 2–3 °C cooler | Shorter | Dark roasts extract faster and burn easier |
| **Dark roast → Medium-light roast** | Finer | 2–3 °C hotter | Longer | Light roasts are denser and need more energy to extract |

---

## Honest Expectation Setting

Not all beans can achieve all flavor targets, regardless of recipe tweaking. When adapting, state the ceiling honestly:

- **Geisha Natural** through a Prismo will be **jammy and sweet** but never as thick as a **Pacamara Black Honey**.
- **Washed Pacás** through a Prismo will be **cleaner and lighter** than a natural processed bean through the same attachment.
- **Kalita Wave Mino** will give you **clarity and consistency** but won't produce the same **bright, separated acidity** as a V60.

Set expectations before brewing. It builds trust and prevents disappointment.
